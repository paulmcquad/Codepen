Paul Allen

American business magnate, investor, philanthropist, and humanitarian
Allen at Flying Heritage Collection in April 2013

    Here's a time line of Paul Allen's life:

  • 1953 - Born in Seattle, Washington, U.S.
  • 1975 - in Albuquerque, New Mexico, Allen and Gates began marketing a BASIC programming language interpreter.
  • 1980 - Allen spearheaded a deal for Microsoft to purchase QDOS (Quick and Dirty Operating System), written by Tim Paterson.
  • 1982 - Allen effectively left Microsoft in 1982 after receiving a Hodgkin's lymphoma diagnosis.
  • 1989 - Allen donated $2 million to the University of Washington to construct the Allen Library, which was named after his father Kenneth S. Allen, a former associate director of the University of Washington library system.
  • 1993 - Allen invested $243 million to acquire 80% of Ticketmaster.
  • 1995 - Allen came up with the original name of "Micro-Soft" (a combination of "microcomputer" and "software"), according to a 1995 Fortune magazine article.
  • 1996 - Allen purchased the Seattle Seahawks NFL team in 1996 when former owner Ken Behring threatened to move the Seahawks to Southern California.
  • 1998 - Allen bought a controlling interest in Charter Communications. Charter filed for bankruptcy reorganization in 2009, with Allen's loss estimated at $7 billion
  • 2000 - Allen officially resigned from his position on the Microsoft board of directors.
  • 2010 - Allen became a signatory of The Giving Pledge, promising to give at least half of his fortune to philanthropic causes.
  • 2013 - Allen opened a new Vulcan Capital office in Palo Alto, California.
  • 2014 - he still held 100 million shares of Microsoft.
  • 2014 - Online philanthropy magazine Inside Philanthropy made Allen their inaugural "Philanthropist of the Year"[96] for his ongoing effort to stop the Ebola outbreak in West Africa.
  • 2017 - Allen donated $40 million (with an additional $10 million added by Microsoft) to reorganize the University of Washington's Computer Science and Engineering department into the Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science and Engineering.
  • 2018 - dies at the age of 65.
